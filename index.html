01 — The Invention

One box, every kind of information.

PocketBox is a literary work first envisioned in 1991: an application combination built on top of an operating system, designed so that a person's entire information life — contacts, receipts, schedules, passwords, photographs — could be saved, retrieved, and secured without spatial or temporal constraint.

Literary Work
No 1 — Root Index

Name of literary work: POCKETBOX. Production history: work envisioned in 1991. Contents: an application combination built on an operating system. Purpose: all of a person's information held in one set of lockers.

The planning behind PocketBox was simple: information should be saved quickly and easily, without spatial or temporal constraint — retrievable no matter when or where, through a graphical interface built to express that structure visually.

Every category of personal record was organized as a numbered "Box" inside a single window — from Address (001) and Schedule (008) to Bank (009), Testament (022), and Real Estate (020) — years before a home screen of app icons became the default way to organize a personal device.

Pocket Box, made available as a CD-ROM title, is an application configuration sold worldwide across today's smart devices without the knowledge of its original author.

04 — Timeline

06 — Frequently Asked

Before you reach out.

What exactly is PocketBox? +

PocketBox is a boxed desktop application, first envisioned in 1991 by CNTI Canada, that organizes a person's entire information life — contacts, schedules, passwords, images, and more — inside a single password-protected interface of numbered "boxes."

What is the basis of the infringement claims? +

CNTI Canada documents specific interface conventions — page-based icon grids, preview arrays, incremental search, schedule tools, drawing tools, and screen locks — that appeared in PocketBox years before they became standard on smart devices.

Can I see the full evidence docket? +
